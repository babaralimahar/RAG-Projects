Title: Chronic Low-Grade Inflammation as a Unifying Mechanism in Insulin Resistance

Abstract: Accumulating evidence implicates chronic low-grade inflammation as a central mechanism linking obesity, insulin resistance, and type 2 diabetes. Activation of the NF-kB and JNK signaling pathways in insulin-responsive tissues leads to serine phosphorylation of insulin receptor substrate-1, impairing downstream insulin signaling and glucose uptake. Elevated circulating levels of inflammatory mediators, including TNF-alpha, IL-1beta, and IL-6, are consistently observed in insulin-resistant states and correlate with markers of metabolic dysfunction. The NLRP3 inflammasome has been identified as a key driver of IL-1beta production in response to metabolic stressors such as elevated free fatty acids and glucose. Anti-inflammatory therapeutic approaches, including IL-1 receptor antagonists and low-dose salicylates, have shown modest improvements in glycemic parameters in clinical trials, providing proof-of-concept support for inflammation as a modifiable target in T2DM management.

Keywords: inflammation, insulin resistance, NF-kB, NLRP3 inflammasome, type 2 diabetes
