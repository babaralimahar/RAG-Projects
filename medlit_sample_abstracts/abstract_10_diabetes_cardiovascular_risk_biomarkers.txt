Title: Emerging Biomarkers for Early Detection of Diabetic Cardiomyopathy

Abstract: Diabetic cardiomyopathy is a distinct clinical entity characterized by myocardial structural and functional abnormalities occurring independently of coronary artery disease or hypertension. Early detection remains challenging due to the often subclinical nature of early disease. This review examines emerging circulating biomarkers, including galectin-3, soluble ST2, and growth differentiation factor-15 (GDF-15), which reflect myocardial fibrosis and cellular stress pathways implicated in diabetic cardiomyopathy. Elevated galectin-3 levels have been associated with increased myocardial fibrosis on cardiac MRI and predict adverse remodeling in longitudinal cohorts of patients with T2DM. Soluble ST2, a marker of mechanical cardiac strain, has shown utility in identifying patients at risk for progression to overt heart failure with preserved ejection fraction. Integration of these novel biomarkers with conventional echocardiographic assessment may enable earlier identification of at-risk patients and timelier initiation of cardioprotective therapies such as SGLT2 inhibitors.

Keywords: diabetic cardiomyopathy, galectin-3, soluble ST2, GDF-15, biomarkers
