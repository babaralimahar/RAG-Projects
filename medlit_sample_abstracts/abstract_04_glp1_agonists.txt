Title: GLP-1 Receptor Agonists: Mechanisms and Cardiovascular Outcomes in Type 2 Diabetes

Abstract: Glucagon-like peptide-1 (GLP-1) receptor agonists have emerged as a cornerstone therapy for type 2 diabetes due to their dual benefits of glycemic control and cardiovascular risk reduction. These agents enhance glucose-dependent insulin secretion, suppress glucagon release, slow gastric emptying, and promote satiety through central nervous system pathways, resulting in significant weight loss. Large cardiovascular outcome trials, including LEADER and SUSTAIN-6, demonstrated significant reductions in major adverse cardiovascular events among patients treated with liraglutide and semaglutide, respectively, independent of glycemic control. Proposed cardioprotective mechanisms include improved endothelial function, reduced inflammation, and beneficial effects on left ventricular remodeling. Given this evidence, current guidelines recommend GLP-1 receptor agonists for patients with T2DM and established atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease, often in combination with metformin as foundational therapy.

Keywords: GLP-1 receptor agonist, semaglutide, liraglutide, cardiovascular outcomes, type 2 diabetes
