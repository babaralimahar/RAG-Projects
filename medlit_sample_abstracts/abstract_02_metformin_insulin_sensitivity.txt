Title: Metformin Improves Insulin Sensitivity Through AMPK-Dependent and AMPK-Independent Pathways

Abstract: Metformin remains the first-line pharmacological therapy for type 2 diabetes due to its favorable efficacy and safety profile. Its primary mechanism of action involves activation of AMP-activated protein kinase (AMPK) in hepatocytes, which suppresses gluconeogenic gene expression and reduces hepatic glucose output. Beyond hepatic effects, metformin enhances peripheral insulin sensitivity by increasing GLUT4 expression and translocation in skeletal muscle, partially through AMPK-independent mechanisms involving mitochondrial complex I inhibition. Recent evidence suggests metformin also alters gut microbiota composition, increasing short-chain fatty acid producing bacteria, which may contribute to improved glucose homeostasis via gut-derived incretin signaling. Clinical trials consistently demonstrate that metformin lowers HbA1c by approximately 1.0-1.5% while having a neutral or favorable effect on body weight, distinguishing it from insulin secretagogues. These findings support metformin's continued role as a foundational therapy in T2DM management.

Keywords: metformin, AMPK, insulin sensitivity, gut microbiota, hepatic glucose output
