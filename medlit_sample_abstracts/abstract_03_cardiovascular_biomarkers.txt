Title: Cardiovascular Biomarkers Predicting Major Adverse Cardiac Events in Patients with Type 2 Diabetes

Abstract: Patients with type 2 diabetes face a two- to four-fold increased risk of cardiovascular disease compared to non-diabetic individuals, making early risk stratification critical. This study evaluated the prognostic value of high-sensitivity cardiac troponin T (hs-cTnT), N-terminal pro-B-type natriuretic peptide (NT-proBNP), and high-sensitivity C-reactive protein (hs-CRP) in predicting major adverse cardiovascular events (MACE) over a five-year follow-up period in a cohort of 2,140 patients with T2DM. Elevated hs-cTnT levels above the 99th percentile were associated with a hazard ratio of 2.3 for MACE, while NT-proBNP levels in the highest tertile conferred a hazard ratio of 1.9. Combining biomarkers improved discriminatory power (C-statistic 0.78) compared to traditional risk factors alone. These results support incorporating cardiac biomarker panels into routine risk assessment for diabetic patients, particularly those with additional risk factors such as albuminuria or long disease duration.

Keywords: cardiovascular biomarkers, troponin, NT-proBNP, type 2 diabetes, major adverse cardiac events
